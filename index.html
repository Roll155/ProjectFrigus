Problem Statement

Addressing critical global challenges in cold storage accessibility

Lack of reliable cold storage is a major global issue affecting food security, healthcare, and sustainable development. Traditional refrigeration requires continuous electricity, which is unavailable in many parts of the world.

  • Food wastage due to inadequate cold storage
  • Vaccine spoilage in remote healthcare facilities
  • High energy costs and carbon footprint of conventional refrigeration
  • Lack of reliable electricity in rural and off-grid areas

Project Frigus addresses these challenges through innovative PCM-based passive cooling technology that requires zero electricity for operation.

Concept & Working Principle

Phase Change Material (PCM) based passive cooling technology

Project Frigus utilizes Phase Change Materials (PCMs) that absorb and release thermal energy during phase transitions, maintaining a stable temperature without external energy input.

How It Works:

  1. PCM is pre-charged (solidified) during cooler periods or using minimal energy
  2. As ambient temperature rises, PCM absorbs heat and begins melting at a precise temperature (4-8°C)
  3. During melting, PCM maintains a constant temperature inside the storage unit
  4. When ambient temperature drops, PCM releases stored heat and solidifies again

This latent heat storage principle provides 72+ hours of consistent cooling without electricity.

PCM State:

Solid Phase

PCM is solidified, storing cooling energy. Temperature remains constant during melting.

Temperature: 4°C

Melting Phase

PCM absorbs heat from the environment, maintaining stable internal temperature.

Temperature: 4-8°C
